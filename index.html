Chapter 1: The Unexpected Guest

An employee in the Human Resources department reports that her computer has slowed down after opening an email attachment named "Resume_CV.docx".

Initial Findings

Email and file analysis reveals that it executed an encoded PowerShell command hidden inside the document.

powershell.exe -NoP -NonI -Exec Bypass -EncodedCommand JABjAGwAaQBlAG4AdA....
Key Takeaway: Phishing emails are the most common entry point for ransomware. Swift isolation is key to preventing lateral movement.

Chapter 3: The Crisis Room & The Ransom Note

Files have begun to encrypt, and a ransom note has appeared on servers. The attackers are demanding $250,000 and threatening to leak the data. Management is in a panic.

Management Emergency Channel

CEO: Our reputation is on the line! Is customer data safe? End this crisis now!
CFO: $250,000! We can't afford that. Can't we restore from our backups?
Legal Counsel: Paying the ransom could mean financing illegal activities and may have legal ramifications.
Key Takeaway: The decision to pay a ransom is not just technical; it has financial, legal, and ethical dimensions.

Chapter 4: The Hidden Threat: Data Exfiltration

While your team deals with the encrypted files, threat hunting tools uncover a more sinister danger: the attackers are actively leaking data from the customer database (Double Extortion)!

SIEM Logs

[11:45:10] CRITICAL: Persistent, encrypted data stream detected from DB-Server (192.168.1.70) to an unknown external IP (81.25.11.92)!
Key Takeaway: Modern ransomware attacks often use "double extortion" tactics, threatening to leak stolen data if the ransom isn't paid.
